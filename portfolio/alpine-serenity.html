Alpine Serenity Estate

Every Last Detail

Project Details

Location: Summit Heights
Property Size: 5 acres
Project Duration: 8 months
Services: Design, Construction, Seasonal Programs

The Challenge

This expansive mountain estate needed a landscape that could provide function, respite, and beauty in challenging terrain. The property featured steep slopes, rocky outcroppings, and dramatic elevation changes that limited usable outdoor space. These natural features were surrounded by neighboring structures and utilities, which constrained our ability to use borrowed views to expand the sense of scale in the landscape.

The homeowners envisioned outdoor living spaces that could accommodate family gatherings, intimate entertaining, and quiet contemplationâ€”all while working with the natural contours of the land rather than against them.

Our Approach

Highland relishes designing challenging spaces and jumped at this opportunity to recapture and command the dramatic views from these mountainside terraces. Our goals were to provide a sense of privacy while creating enough interest in materials and plantings to add warmth and wonder to the views throughout.

Design Philosophy

We approached this project with three core principles:

  • Work with the terrain: Rather than fighting the natural slope, we created a series of terraced levels that follow the land's natural contours.
  • Native integration: Select alpine plants and materials indigenous to the region to ensure sustainability and year-round beauty.
  • Functional artistry: Every element serves both a practical purpose and contributes to the overall aesthetic vision.

Key Features

Natural Stone Terracing

We constructed five distinct terraced levels using locally-sourced granite and sandstone. Each terrace serves a specific purpose while maintaining visual continuity throughout the property. The dry-stacked retaining walls, some reaching 8 feet in height, required specialized engineering and master stonework to ensure structural integrity on the challenging slope.

Alpine Garden Rooms

The property now features four distinct garden "rooms," each with its own character:

  • Entry Garden: Drought-tolerant alpines and ornamental grasses create a low-maintenance welcome that thrives in full sun.
  • Woodland Shade Garden: Native ferns, hostas, and bleeding hearts flourish under the existing pine canopy.
  • Meditation Garden: A serene space with Japanese-inspired elements including a small contemplative pool and carefully placed boulders.
  • Wildflower Meadow: A naturalized area with native wildflowers that attract pollinators and provide seasonal color.

Water Features

A custom-designed waterfall cascades through three of the terraced levels, creating both visual interest and soothing sound throughout the property. The recirculating system uses natural filtration and includes:

  • 18-foot main waterfall with multi-tiered cascades
  • Natural stone streambed winding through gardens
  • Two reflection pools with aquatic plantings
  • Integrated lighting for nighttime ambiance

Outdoor Living Spaces

We created three distinct outdoor living areas to accommodate different activities and group sizes:

  • Main Terrace (1,200 sq ft): Features a custom stone fireplace, built-in seating, and dining area for 12+ people.
  • Morning Coffee Nook: An intimate east-facing space with sunrise views and built-in bench seating.
  • Fire Pit Gathering Area: A circular design with custom stone seating for evening gatherings under the stars.

Planting Design

The planting palette was carefully curated to thrive at 8,500 feet elevation while providing three-season interest. We selected 47 different plant varieties including:

Trees & Shrubs

  • Quaking Aspen (Populus tremuloides)
  • Bristlecone Pine (Pinus aristata)
  • Red Twig Dogwood (Cornus sericea)
  • Mountain Mahogany (Cercocarpus montanus)

Perennials

  • Alpine Columbine (Aquilegia caerulea)
  • Blanket Flower (Gaillardia)
  • Penstemon varieties
  • Lupine (Lupinus)

Grasses & Groundcovers

  • Blue Grama Grass (Bouteloua gracilis)
  • Kinnikinnick (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi)
  • Creeping Thyme (Thymus serpyllum)
  • Sedum varieties

Sustainability Features

Environmental stewardship was central to this project. We implemented several sustainable practices:

  • Rainwater harvesting: 2,500-gallon underground cistern collects roof runoff for irrigation.
  • Drip irrigation: Water-efficient system delivers moisture directly to plant roots, reducing waste by 60%.
  • Permeable hardscaping: All pathways use permeable materials allowing natural water infiltration.
  • Native plants: 85% of plantings are Colorado natives requiring minimal supplemental water once established.
  • LED lighting: Energy-efficient landscape lighting throughout.

Challenges Overcome

Access & Equipment

The steep terrain and remote location required creative solutions. We utilized specialized excavation equipment designed for hillside work and, in some areas, hand-carried materials to protect established vegetation and minimize site disturbance.

Soil Amendments

The native soil was extremely rocky with minimal organic content. We amended over 3,000 cubic yards of soil, adding compost and specialized mountain soil blends to create optimal growing conditions for our plant selections.

Drainage Engineering

Managing water flow on a steep slope required sophisticated drainage solutions. We installed French drains, catch basins, and carefully engineered grading to direct water away from structures while feeding the water features and irrigation system.

The Result

The transformation took eight months and involved a team of 12 specialists including landscape architects, structural engineers, master stonemasons, and horticulturists. The result is a stunning mountain estate that seamlessly blends luxury outdoor living with the natural beauty of the Colorado Rockies.

The homeowners now enjoy a property that functions beautifully for both large gatherings and quiet family moments. The terraced design created over 4,000 square feet of usable outdoor space where previously there was only steep, unusable hillside. The gardens provide year-round interest, the water features add ambiance, and the outdoor living areas have become the heart of the home.

Every detail was thoughtfully considered, from the placement of individual stones to the selection of plants that thrive at our elevation. Two years later, it still takes our breath away every single day."
